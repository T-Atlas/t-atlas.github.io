## 🌟 1. About the Lab

FinD Lab is part of the <span class="findlab-highlight">Key Laboratory of Intelligent Information Processing</span>, Institute of Computing Technology (ICT), Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS). It is also affiliated with the <span class="findlab-highlight">State Key Laboratory of AI Safety</span>, ICT, CAS, Beijing, China. The team has long worked at the intersection of <span class="findlab-emphasis">artificial intelligence and financial security</span>.

![FinD Lab @ ICT](/images/find-lab-wechat/find-lab-logo.png){: .findlab-wide-image }

<div class="findlab-topic" markdown="1">
<span class="findlab-topic-title">🚀 Core Focus</span>
The team focuses on the central question of <span class="findlab-emphasis">how AI can understand and safeguard complex financial systems</span>. Centered on financial risk modeling driven by user online behavior, the group continues to publish high-quality research in top data mining and natural language processing venues, including <span class="findlab-highlight">IEEE TKDE, KDD, WWW, SIGIR, and ACL</span>.
</div>

<div class="findlab-topic" markdown="1">
<span class="findlab-topic-title">👥 Team Scale</span>
The team currently includes one professor, one assistant professor, one postdoctoral researcher, and more than 20 Ph.D. and master's students. The group maintains an open and collaborative atmosphere. In recent years, multiple members have been selected for talent programs such as the Beijing Outstanding Young Scientists Program, the Beijing Nova Program, and the CAS Youth Innovation Promotion Association.
</div>

<div class="findlab-topic" markdown="1">
<span class="findlab-topic-title">💼 Real-World Deployment</span>
The team's technologies have been deployed at scale in key institutions and companies, including the <span class="findlab-highlight">Shenzhen Stock Exchange, Ant Group, Alibaba, Tencent, and ByteDance</span>. They have created practical impact in scenarios such as security in digital inclusive finance, securities-market risk monitoring, and transaction fraud prevention.

## 🔍 2. Research Directions

Driven by major national needs in financial security, the lab focuses on two core questions:

<ul class="findlab-core-list">
  <li><span class="findlab-highlight">AI for Financial Security:</span> how to use AI to build stronger capabilities for financial risk perception and prevention.</li>
  <li><span class="findlab-highlight">AI Safety in Finance:</span> how to ensure the safety and reliability of AI in high-stakes financial scenarios.</li>
</ul>

On this foundation, the lab has formed three mutually reinforcing research directions:

<div class="findlab-topic" markdown="1">
<span class="findlab-topic-title">📍 1. Behavior Data × Knowledge-Driven Intelligent Risk Modeling (Micro to Meso)</span>
For real-world financial scenarios characterized by high noise, strong adversarial behavior, and weak labels, the team studies user behavior modeling, multimodal information fusion, and knowledge-enhanced learning to improve individual and group-level risk identification.
</div>

<div class="findlab-topic" markdown="1">
<span class="findlab-topic-title">📍 2. AI-Driven Risk Reasoning and Cross-Domain Evolution Modeling (Meso to Macro)</span>
To understand how risks evolve from signals into events and then spread further, the team studies risk propagation mechanisms, causal reasoning, and situation projection, aiming to build interpretable and simulatable frameworks for macro-level risk analysis.
</div>

<div class="findlab-topic" markdown="1">
<span class="findlab-topic-title">📍 3. Safety Evaluation and Enhancement of Financial Intelligence Models (Model Level)</span>
For problems such as model failure, adversarial attacks, and decision bias in financial scenarios, the team develops systematic safety evaluation frameworks and enhancement mechanisms, helping financial AI move from being merely usable to being trustworthy.
</div>

💡 In short, we care not only about whether models are effective, but also whether they are <span class="findlab-highlight">reliable, interpretable, and deployable</span> in the real, complex world.
